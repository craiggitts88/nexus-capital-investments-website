THE JOURNEY

How Nexus Algorithmics
Came to Be

Born from years of active trading, algorithm development, and a relentless pursuit of operational precision.

The Beginning
Manual Trading & Market Foundations
Craig Gittins and Usama Azim met through their trading mentor while learning Wyckoff theory. Through extensive manual trading, they built deep market knowledge — understanding price action, orderflow, and the psychological demands of active trading.
The Pivot
From Manual to Systematic
Recognising that the psychological burden of manual trading was a structural disadvantage, the founders began developing algorithmic systems. Their first algorithm — "White Rabbit" — was coded in MQ4 and MQ5, based on statistical research from published trader Tom Hougaard.
The Platforms
MetaTrader, Python & Beyond
Deep engagement with platforms such as MetaTrader 5, ATAS and Python became central to the founders' trading operations. The desire to build precision tools and algorithms for these platform planted the seed for what would become Nexus Algorithmics.
The Division
Nexus Algorithmics is Founded
The technology and research arm of Nexus Capital & Investments is established — with a clear mandate: build the professional tools that active traders actually need. The Nexus Position Tool becomes the first product for the ATAS Platform, solving a real problem the founders faced in their own trading every day.
The Future
Quant Panel & Beyond
Nexus Algorithmics continues to build. The Quant Panel is in active development — a full quantitative analytics suite designed to bring institutional-grade intelligence to the individual trader's screen. Python-based algorithm development and market-neutral strategies are also on the roadmap for the Nexus Capital trading division.
