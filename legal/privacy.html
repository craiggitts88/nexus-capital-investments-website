Nexus Capital & Investments ("we", "us", "our"), trading as and through its algorithmic division Nexus Algorithmics, is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and your rights in relation to it.

This policy applies to all visitors to our website and all purchasers of our software products. We comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who We Are

Data Controller: Nexus Capital & Investments (Nexus Algorithmics)

For data protection enquiries please use the contact details at the bottom of this page.

2. Data We Collect

We collect only the minimum personal data necessary to process your purchase and provide support. The categories of data we may collect include:

Category Examples Source
Identity data First name, last name Provided by you at checkout
Contact data Email address Provided by you at checkout
Transaction data Purchase details, licence key, order reference Generated at purchase via LemonSqueezy
Technical data Browser type, device type, approximate location (country) Collected automatically when visiting our website
Communications data Support emails and enquiries Provided by you when contacting us

We do not collect or store payment card details. Payment information is handled exclusively by our payment processor, LemonSqueezy.

We do not collect any special category data (as defined by UK GDPR, Article 9) and do not knowingly collect data from individuals under the age of 18.

3. How We Use Your Data

We process your personal data on the following legal bases and for the following purposes:

Purpose Legal Basis
Processing your purchase and delivering digital products Performance of a contract (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(b))
Sending your download link and licence information Performance of a contract (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(b))
Responding to support requests Performance of a contract / Legitimate interests
Maintaining records for legal and tax compliance Legal obligation (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(c))
Notifying you of significant product updates (opt-in) Consent (UK GDPR Art. 6(1)(a))

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for marketing purposes. We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

4. Third-Party Service Providers

We share your data with the following trusted third-party processors only to the extent necessary to fulfil your purchase and support your use of our products:

  • LemonSqueezy — payment processing, order management, and digital product delivery. LemonSqueezy acts as a merchant of record and processes your payment data under their own privacy policy. View it at lemonsqueezy.com/privacy.
  • GitHub Pages — hosting of this website. No personal data is submitted to GitHub through use of our public website.
  • Google Fonts — font delivery. Google may log the IP address of your request. See Google's privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

All third-party processors are required to process your data only on our instructions and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

5. Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses only essential technical cookies necessary for the website to function. We do not use advertising, analytics, or tracking cookies. Specifically:

  • We do not use Google Analytics or any other analytics platform
  • We do not use Facebook Pixel or any social media tracking
  • We do not serve third-party advertising

LemonSqueezy may set cookies on their checkout pages when you complete a purchase. These are governed by LemonSqueezy's own cookie policy.

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies will not affect your ability to browse our website.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law:

  • Purchase records — retained for 7 years to comply with UK tax and accounting obligations
  • Support communications — retained for up to 2 years after resolution
  • Website technical data — not stored beyond your browsing session

When data is no longer required it will be securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

As a UK data subject you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your data where there is no overriding legal basis for retention
  • Right to restrict processing — request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe we have not handled your data lawfully.

8. Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, loss, or destruction. Our website is served over HTTPS and payment processing is handled by PCI-compliant third parties.

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. If you believe your data has been compromised, please contact us immediately.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Significant changes will be communicated by email where we hold your contact details.
